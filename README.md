# Customer Segmentation using RFM Model

## Overview

Customer segmentation is one of the most powerful techniques used by businesses to understand customer behavior, improve retention, and maximize revenue. This project applies the **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)** framework to classify customers into meaningful segments and provide actionable business recommendations.

Using customer transaction data, the project identifies high-value customers, loyal customers, potential loyalists, at-risk customers, and lost customers, enabling organizations to design targeted marketing campaigns and improve customer engagement.

---

## Business Problem

Businesses often struggle to answer critical questions:

* Who are our most valuable customers?
* Which customers contribute the highest revenue?
* Which customers are likely to churn?
* How can marketing campaigns be personalized?
* Which customer groups require retention efforts?

This project addresses these challenges through data-driven customer segmentation using the RFM methodology.

## RFM Framework

### Recency (R)

Measures how recently a customer made a purchase.

**Business Meaning:**
Customers who purchased recently are more likely to respond to future campaigns.

### Frequency (F)

Measures how often a customer purchases.

**Business Meaning:**
Frequent buyers generally have higher engagement and loyalty.

### Monetary (M)

Measures how much money a customer spends.

**Business Meaning:**
Higher-spending customers contribute more to business revenue.

## Customer Segments

The project categorizes customers into the following groups:

### Champions

Most valuable customers with high recency, frequency, and spending.

### Loyal Customers

Frequent purchasers with strong engagement.

### Potential Loyalists

Customers showing good engagement and future potential.

### New Customers

Recently acquired customers.

### At-Risk Customers

Previously active customers whose engagement has declined.

### Lost Customers

Customers with little or no recent activity.

### Low Engagement Customers

Customers with low purchase frequency and low spending.

## Key Business Insights

* Champions generate the highest share of revenue despite representing a smaller customer base.
* Loyal Customers contribute significantly to repeat purchases and long-term revenue.
* Potential Loyalists offer strong upselling and cross-selling opportunities.
* At-Risk Customers require immediate retention efforts.
* Lost Customers can be targeted through win-back campaigns.
* Revenue concentration highlights the importance of high-value customer retention.
